What are the signs of Depression?

Usually a lot of people get depression and sadness mixed up. Making very false assumptions that they have depression or someone else.

Here are the signs of depression that you should be aware of:

  • You are always tired; always have low energy and you always struggle to accomplish everything on your daily "to do" list.

  • It's hard to find the motivation to do anything... including going out with friends or completing tasks

  • You feel anxious and worried about little things in life

  • Negative thoughts and feelings of anger are often playing themselves in your mind

  • You feel restless, irritable and often lash out at the people who are closest to you

  • It can be difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep, regardless of how tired you may be

  • You can't concentrate on anything; your mind feels like jelly and you are more forgetful than ever

  • You have headaches, backaches, joint pain and other physical ailments that you can’t pin-point the problem and don’t respond to regular treatments
  • Even when there is a room full of people around you... you still feel lonely and often worry that no one really likes you

  • You have problems with your weight, and you are starting to feel overwhelmed by your own life.
If you have these symptoms it might be good to check in with a medical professional and seek help immediately, or try talking to a trusted adult.

Here are 8 different ways that you might want to try out:

  1. Attend therpy sessions-
    You might what to consider taking therapy or counselling session. This doesn't work for everyone, especially for those people who chose to keep their thoughts and problems to themselves. Sharing your story, might help you and make you a little stronger.

  2. Get enough sleep-
    Sleep can help with great mood disorders. So it's important to keep a consistent sleep and wake cycle along with maintaining healthy sleeping habits. Lots of people who have depression say that less sleep can worsen depression and so can too much sleep.

  4. Eat well-
    We know that nourishing our bodies with vitamins and minerals is the key to our health. The same is true for depression. In fact, poor nutrition can make it worse.

  5. Stay connected to people-
    Social isolation is your worst enemy. If you're having a difficult time connecting with others, volunteer, or join a online group with like-minded people. You can always do something fun with your family members. Try going out too.

  6. Avoid people who are toxic to you-
    They may be envious, judgmental and competitive. If you stop seeing these people, and limit your exposure, it'll benefit you. Surround yourself with people that make you happy and that accept you for you. As a teenage girl, school, bullying and social media can affect your mentality a lot. So put your phone away and spend time with people that will affectionate towards you.

  7. Be nice to yourself-
    I know this one isn't easy. We're so hard on ourselves. Start paying attention to the words, phrases, thoughts bouncing around in your head. Most of us have hardly anything good to think or say about ourselves. Stop it. Start being nice to yourself. Practice thinking those thoughts. Be nice to yourself.

  8. Do things for you-
    As teenage girls some of us tend to do things for other people, to impress others, or to make others happy. But stop, start to do things that will benefit YOU! Do thing that'll make you happy as an individual.
